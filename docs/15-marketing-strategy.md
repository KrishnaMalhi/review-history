# ReviewHistory — Marketing Strategy

---

## 🧠 Core Principle

> **Think like a Pakistani consumer. Go where they already are.**

Pakistani consumers don't search Google for "tenant review platform." They ask their friends on WhatsApp. They browse Facebook community groups. They watch TikTok videos. They trust personal recommendations above all else.

ReviewHistory's marketing strategy is built around these realities — not Western digital marketing playbooks.

---

## 🔁 3 Built-In Viral Loops

These are not marketing campaigns. These are features baked into the product itself.

### Viral Loop 1: WhatsApp Share Warning

Every entity page has a one-tap "Share on WhatsApp" button that generates a pre-written Urdu warning message with a link.

```
1 person shares warning to a 200-person WhatsApp group
        ↓
10–20 people click the link (5–10% CTR)
        ↓
3–5 sign up and leave their own review
        ↓
Their reviews get shared by others
        ↓
Platform grows with zero cost per acquisition
```

**Expected k-factor (viral coefficient):** 1.2–1.8 in first year
(Every 1 user brings in 1.2–1.8 new users through shares)

### Viral Loop 2: SEO Magnet

Every entity page is SEO-optimised and indexed by Google within days of creation.

**Long-tail search targets:**
- "Dr. Ahmed Johar Town review"
- "Khalid Property Dealer Karachi complaints"
- "Rizwan Mechanic Gulberg genuine parts"

**Why this works:**
- 40% of people search for a specific person or business name before dealing with them
- No competitor currently owns these long-tail keywords in Pakistan
- Each new entity = new SEO page = new traffic source

**Target:** 10,000+ organic monthly visitors by Month 6

### Viral Loop 3: Entity Page Sharing

Verified business owners share their own entity page to demonstrate their high trust score.

```
Business owner (with Trust Score 85) shares:
"Check our reviews on ReviewHistory: [link]"
        ↓
Customers visit the page
        ↓
Some leave reviews (even more positive)
        ↓
Score improves further
        ↓
Owner shares more
```

This turns satisfied businesses into organic marketers.

## 🏘️ Phase 1: Hyper-Local Launch (Month 1–2)

**Start city: Lahore**  
**Start area: Johar Town**

**Why hyper-local?**
- Johar Town is one of Lahore's densest residential areas with active tenant communities
- 200,000+ residents in close proximity
- Active WhatsApp tenant groups already exist
- Success in one area creates proof of concept for expansion

### Johar Town Launch Plan

1. **Personally add 50 entities** — top doctors, mechanics, shops, landlords known to be problematic
2. **Write first 30 reviews** from personal experience or gathered from friends
3. **Share in 10 WhatsApp groups** (tenant groups, alumni groups, area community groups)
4. **Post in 3 Facebook groups:** "Johar Town Residents," "Lahore Tenants," "Lahore Consumer Rights"
5. **Post 1 TikTok video:** "Warning: This landlord in Johar Town hasn't returned a single deposit"

**Success criteria for Phase 1:** 500 users, 200 reviews, from Johar Town alone, within 2 months.

University students are ReviewHistory's most valuable early adopters. They:
- Need to rent apartments (landlord reviews)
- Use doctors (overcharging awareness)

## 🏆 Market Leader Strategy

The goal is not to be "a review platform in Pakistan." The goal is to be **the** reference platform Pakistanis check before making any local transaction.

**Milestones to market leadership:**

1. **10,000 users** — Become the undisputed top result for "landlord review Pakistan"
2. **50,000 users** — Media coverage. PR stories. National recognition.
3. **100,000 users** — B2B partnership conversations open naturally
4. **500,000 users** — Series A fundraising. National brand. Household name.

**The network effect protects the market position:** At 100,000 reviews, no competitor can replicate the data in less than 2 years. First-mover advantage is permanent at that scale.
# 15 — Marketing Strategy

## 1. Core Growth Idea

This product grows when users warn other users. That means distribution should be built around:
- search
- sharing
- local relevance
- category pain
- public trust pages

## 2. Best Early Channels

- SEO
- WhatsApp shares
- TikTok / short video explainers
- Facebook groups
- Reddit/local forums where relevant
- local micro-influencer explainers
- city/category landing pages

## 3. Viral Loops

### Loop A — warning share loop
User finds risky entity -> shares profile on WhatsApp -> recipients click -> some search their own entities

### Loop B — add missing entity loop
User cannot find entity -> adds it -> shares it -> others contribute

### Loop C — claim/reply loop
Business notices page -> claims profile -> shares official response -> drives attention

## 4. Content Strategy

Publish pages and content like:
- “Top warning signs before renting in Karachi”
- “How to verify a mechanic before repair”
- “Questions to ask before visiting a clinic”
- “How trust scores work”
- “How to avoid fake reviews”

## 5. TikTok / Reels Strategy

Video themes:
- real-life trust failures
- before-you-rent checklists
- how to read warning tags
- side-by-side category examples
- “search before you pay” concept

## 6. WhatsApp Growth Design

- one-click share buttons
- good Open Graph previews
- short share text templates
- city/locality references in previews

## 7. Launch Strategy

### City-first
Launch in Karachi first or one focused city/area cluster.

### Category-first
Landlords + mechanics + doctors is a strong mix:
- frequent need
- high frustration
- strong sharing behavior

## 8. Brand Messaging

- “Check first, regret less”
- “Search before you rent, visit, hire, or buy”
- “Public trust for real-world decisions”
