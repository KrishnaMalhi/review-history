# ReviewHistory — Product Overview

## 🎯 Vision Statement

Pakistan's first hyper-local, community-driven trust and review platform where people share honest experiences about landlords, doctors, mechanics, shops, and any local entity — warning others and promoting accountability across Pakistan.

**Tagline:** *"ReviewHistory — جاننا آپ کا حق ہے"* (Knowing is Your Right)

---

## 🔥 The Problem

### Real Problems Pakistanis Face Daily

**1. Tenant Problems (Landlords)**
- Landlords keep security deposits without reason after tenants leave
- Hidden charges appear after moving in (maintenance fees, parking fees, extra utility charges)
- Maintenance requests ignored for months — leaking roofs, broken pipes, no gas
- Illegal evictions without notice or legal process
- No platform exists to warn the next tenant

**2. Patient Problems (Doctors)**
- Doctors charge 3–5x the normal market rate at private clinics
- Unnecessary tests prescribed to earn commission from labs
- 2–3 hour wait times with appointments that mean nothing
- Misdiagnosis with no accountability
- Rude, dismissive behavior toward patients

**3. Consumer Problems (Mechanics, Shops)**
- Auto mechanics install cheap/fake/used parts but charge for new genuine ones
- Shops sell expired products, fake brands, short-measure items
- Overcharging customers who don't know the correct market price
- No platform to warn others in the community

**4. Property Dealer Fraud**
- Fake property listings to get people in the office
- Taking advance payment from both buyer and seller (double dealing)
- Disappearing after collecting advance payments
- Pakistan loses billions annually to property fraud

**The Core Problem:** There is NO reliable, hyper-local platform in Pakistan where ordinary people can share honest experiences and warn others before they get scammed.

---

## ✅ The Solution

ReviewHistory allows:

1. **Anyone** to add any entity (landlord, doctor, shop, mechanic) — entity does NOT need an account
2. **Verified users** (phone OTP — 1 SIM = 1 account) to leave honest reviews
3. **Community** to vote on review helpfulness (helpful/fake voting)
4. **Trust scores** (0–100) to give instant danger/safe signals
5. **Warning tags** to surface common complaints at a glance
6. **WhatsApp sharing** to spread warnings virally across Pakistan

## 🔑 Key Differentiators

1. **Entity doesn't need an account** — A landlord doesn't need to register. Users add them.
2. **Location fingerprint** — Name + City + Area + Category distinguishes "Dr. Ahmed" in Johar Town from "Dr. Ahmed" in DHA
3. **Community-powered moderation** — Users vote reviews helpful/fake, reducing platform bias
4. **Pakistan-specific** — Urdu support, local categories, NADRA SIM = verified identity
5. **WhatsApp-first sharing** — Built for Pakistani social behavior where WhatsApp groups are the primary communication channel

# 01 — Product Overview

## 1. Vision

Build the most trusted public experience graph for Pakistan: a platform where users can search, review, warn, and evaluate people, businesses, and services before making a real-world decision.

## 2. Problem

In many everyday categories, users rely on weak trust signals:
- landlords hide deposit abuse
- clinics and small service providers have no reliable review history
- mechanics, agencies, tutors, and local businesses are often discovered through word of mouth only
- review signals are fragmented across WhatsApp, Facebook groups, Google Maps, and personal networks
- fake praise and fake attacks distort reality

Users do not need only ratings. They need:
- repeated complaint patterns
- category-specific warnings
- trust scores
- verified signals
- right of reply
- searchable history

## 3. Product Statement

A community-driven platform where:
- anyone can add an entity
- anyone can review an entity
- the entity does not need to create an account first
- users can search by person, business, area, or category
- the system detects duplicates, fraud patterns, and suspicious reviews
- reviewed entities can claim profiles and publicly respond, but cannot delete valid criticism

## 4. Primary Use Cases

### 4.1 User checks a landlord before renting
User searches name, phone, area, or property. They see:
- trust score
- repeated warnings
- review volume
- replies from owner
- suspicious-review indicators

### 4.2 User adds a doctor/business that is missing
User creates a community entity with:
- name
- city
- area
- category
- optional phone
- optional address / landmark

### 4.3 User leaves a review after a bad experience
User submits:
- rating
- review text
- category-specific warning tags
- optional proof metadata
- visit/tenancy/service timeframe
- helpfulness context

### 4.4 Reviewed entity claims their profile
Entity verifies ownership by phone or manual process and gets:
- right to reply
- right to flag abuse
- profile improvement privileges within policy limits
- analytics and premium tools later

## 5. Why This Can Work

This model has been validated globally:
- Glassdoor: company reviews without company control
- Trustpilot: review any business
- Yelp: anyone can review local businesses
- RateMyProfessor: teachers reviewed without teacher-owned profiles
- ApartmentRatings: public apartment and landlord feedback

The local gap is category breadth plus Pakistan-specific trust design.

## 6. Differentiators

### 6.1 Pakistan-first trust mechanics
- phone OTP with Pakistani numbers
- Urdu/English warning tags
- area and locality-aware search
- category-specific abuse patterns

### 6.2 More than star ratings
- trust score
- warning intensity
- repeat allegations summary
- suspicious-review labels
- owner/public replies
- review quality weighting

### 6.3 Entity-first discovery
Bad actors do not need to onboard for the market to work.

## 9. Operating Model

The platform must be operated as a neutral review host, not as a publisher endorsing every claim. This means:
- clear content rules
- fair moderation process
- right of reply
- documented takedown paths
- transparent trust scoring
- auditable moderation decisions
