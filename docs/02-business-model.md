# ReviewHistory — Business Model

## 💡 Core Principle

**Free until 10,000+ users.** Revenue kills growth if introduced too early. Trust first. Community first. Money second.

---

## 💰 Revenue Streams

### Stream 1: Verified Business Badge (Month 6+)

- **What:** Businesses pay to show a "Verified & Responsive Owner" badge
- **Price:** PKR 1,500/month (~$5.50)
- **Value to business:** Builds customer confidence, shows they care, stands out from unverified listings
- **Acquisition:** Notify top-rated entities (Trust Score 70+) via in-app message — no cold calling
- **Projections:**
  - Month 6: 30 businesses × PKR 1,500 = PKR 45,000/month
  - Month 9: 100 businesses × PKR 1,500 = PKR 150,000/month
  - Year 1: 300 businesses × PKR 1,500 = PKR 450,000/month

### Stream 2: Featured Listing (Month 6+)

- **What:** Top-rated entity appears first in search results for their category + city
- **Price:** PKR 2,000/month per category per city
- **Example:** "Top Doctor in Johar Town, Lahore" slot
- **Projections:**
  - Month 6: 20 listings × PKR 2,000 = PKR 40,000/month
  - Year 1: 100 listings × PKR 2,000 = PKR 200,000/month

### Stream 3: Reputation Dashboard (Year 1+)

- **What:** Analytics for claimed businesses — review trends, sentiment, competitor comparison
- **Price:** PKR 3,000/month
- **Target:** Doctors, property agencies, clinics, mid-size shops
- **Projections:**
  - Year 1: 50 businesses × PKR 3,000 = PKR 150,000/month
  - Year 2: 200 businesses × PKR 3,000 = PKR 600,000/month

### Stream 4: B2B API / Data Licensing (Year 2+)

- **What:** Property portals embed ReviewHistory trust scores in their listings
- **Potential Partners:** Zameen.com, OLX Pakistan, Rozee.pk, Daraz
- **Price:** Custom enterprise contracts
- **Projection:** PKR 500,000+/month per major partner

### Stream 5: Premium User Plan (Year 1+)

- **What:** Power users pay for advanced features
- **Price:** PKR 299/month
- **Features:** Notifications for new reviews on saved entities, export data as PDF, see review analytics, priority support
- **Projection:** 500 users × PKR 299 = PKR 149,500/month

### JazzCash Integration Flow

```
1. User selects JazzCash on checkout
2. Enter JazzCash mobile number
3. Receive USSD push notification
4. Confirm payment with JazzCash PIN
5. Webhook confirms payment to ReviewHistory API
6. Badge/subscription activated immediately
```

### EasyPaisa Integration Flow

```
1. User selects EasyPaisa on checkout
2. Redirected to EasyPaisa payment page
3. OTP sent to registered number
4. OTP confirmed → payment processed
5. Callback URL notifies ReviewHistory API
6. Subscription activated
```

# 02 — Business Model

## 1. Business Thesis

The platform becomes valuable because trust information is scarce and decision pain is high. Revenue comes from distribution, reputation tools, discovery placement, premium utilities, and B2B trust data products.

## 2. Revenue Streams

### 2.1 Claimed profile subscriptions
Paid plans for businesses / professionals / agencies / landlords:
- profile claim and verification badge
- analytics dashboard
- public reply tools
- lead capture
- profile media
- category highlights
- dispute tracking panel

### Example Pakistan-first pricing
- Basic: PKR 1,500/month
- Growth: PKR 4,500/month
- Pro: PKR 12,000/month

### 2.2 Sponsored placements
Limited and clearly labeled promoted listings for:
- clinics
- mechanics
- agencies
- tutors
- businesses
- service providers

Rule: sponsorship never edits trust score or suppresses negative history.

### 2.3 Local performance ads
- WhatsApp click ads
- call ads
- appointment ads
- quote request ads

### 2.4 Premium consumer tools
Optional user subscriptions:
- advanced filters
- saved alerts
- neighborhood watchlists
- trust change alerts
- deep comparison views
- exportable reports for rentals or vendor due diligence

### 2.5 Verification services
Paid optional verification:
- business verification
- branch verification
- identity + compliance checks for claimants
- document-verified badges

### 2.6 B2B trust intelligence
Sell dashboards or API access to:
- property platforms
- marketplaces
- lead-gen platforms
- insurers
- lenders
- hiring or vendor assessment companies

## 3. Monetization Guardrails

Revenue must not compromise trust:
- no paid review removal
- no paid score boosting
- no shadow suppression of criticism
- no pay-to-hide warning patterns
- no paid editing of public history

## 4. Early Revenue Order

### Phase 1
No monetization or very light monetization
- focus on trust and content supply

### Phase 2
Claimed profile + analytics

### Phase 3
Promoted listings + local ads

### Phase 4
Premium consumer features + B2B trust data

## 5. Cost Structure

### 5.1 Main costs
- cloud hosting
- SMS OTP costs
- storage/CDN
- moderation operations
- legal consultation
- search infrastructure
- monitoring/logging
- support

### 5.2 Biggest early variable cost
OTP and moderation.

## 6. Unit Economics Thinking

The product can work if:
- user acquisition is mostly organic
- local discovery and sharing create repeat traffic
- claimed profiles convert after trust pages start ranking in search
- moderation cost per review falls over time with automation

## 7. Break-even Illustration

This is a planning model, not a guarantee.

### Simple scenario
- 300 paid profiles at PKR 1,500 = PKR 450,000/month
- 80 paid profiles at PKR 4,500 = PKR 360,000/month
- local ads/sponsored placements = PKR 250,000/month
- premium users = PKR 90,000/month

**Illustrative monthly revenue:** PKR 1,150,000

If ops + hosting + support + moderation stay below this, early break-even becomes possible.

## 8. Revenue Projection Framework

### Month 0-6
- no or minimal revenue
- build inventory, SEO, share loops, trust

### Month 6-12
- early claim plans
- local sales outreach
- category-specific pilots

### Year 2
- multiple city coverage
- performance ad network
- consumer premium tools
- API partnerships

## 9. Why Businesses Will Pay

They pay for:
- visibility
- control of their public profile presentation
- reputation monitoring
- ability to publicly respond
- analytics and lead capture
- category differentiation

They should not pay for:
- deleting criticism
- altering trust score
- hiding warnings

## 10. Defensibility

Defensibility comes from:
- local entity graph
- review corpus
- trust score history
- fraud graph
- locality + category knowledge
- user behavior network
- search footprint
