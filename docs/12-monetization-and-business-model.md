# Monetization and Business Model Documentation

## 1. Core Business Principle

Do not destroy trust to monetize.  
If users think money can buy reputation suppression, the platform loses its value.

## 2. Revenue Streams

## 2.1 Claimed Profile Subscription
For businesses/professionals/landlords who claim profiles:
- reply tools
- profile enrichment
- analytics
- lead/contact management
- basic reputation dashboard
- notification tools

This is the cleanest monetization path.

## 2.2 Premium Reputation Analytics
For power users or businesses:
- review trends
- category benchmarks
- sentiment summaries later
- issue frequency tracking
- branch comparison later
- response performance

## 2.3 Sponsored Profiles / Promoted Placement
Only if clearly labeled:
- sponsored badges
- promoted discovery slots
- category sponsorships

Rule:
Sponsored placement must never look like organic trust ranking.

## 2.4 Lead Generation / Contact Tools
For certain categories:
- booking/contact buttons
- inquiry forms
- callback requests
- quote requests

Useful for:
- clinics
- service providers
- rentals
- agencies

## 2.5 B2B Reputation Tools
Longer-term:
- widgets/API for websites
- review monitoring dashboard
- location/branch management
- enterprise moderation inbox

## 2.6 Local Ads
Contextual ad inventory:
- moving services near landlord/rental pages
- repair services near building pages
- insurance/legal/cleaning services where relevant

Use carefully to avoid spammy experience.

## 3. What Not to Sell

Never sell:
- review deletion,
- score manipulation,
- hidden demotion of competitors,
- removal of valid negative reviews,
- fake verification badges.

These destroy the business long term.

## 4. Monetization Timing

### MVP
No aggressive monetization. Focus on trust and content.

### Early post-MVP
Introduce:
- claim subscriptions,
- simple owner dashboard,
- optional promoted profiles with labels.

### Growth stage
Introduce:
- analytics,
- lead tools,
- team accounts,
- APIs.

## 5. Pricing Logic

A simple pricing ladder:
- Free: claim + basic reply
- Pro: analytics + alerts + richer profile
- Business: team tools + lead tools + advanced insights

For landlords or micro businesses, a low local-price plan may work better than global SaaS pricing.

## 6. User Acquisition Advantage

This product can monetize because it creates high-intent discovery:
- people search before spending money,
- people search before renting,
- people search before visiting,
- people search before trusting.

High-intent traffic is monetizable if trust is preserved.

## 7. SEO and Distribution Value

Each entity page, area page, and category page can become:
- search traffic entry point,
- social share target,
- trust comparison page,
- local recommendation asset.

This increases long-term organic acquisition.

## 8. Why Businesses Will Pay

Honest businesses and professionals will pay for:
- visibility,
- analytics,
- response workflow,
- reputation monitoring,
- lead capture,
- profile quality.

They will not pay sustainably for a platform users do not trust.

## 9. 12-Month Business Focus

### Months 0–3
- trust and moderation
- category focus
- SEO basics
- launch community

### Months 3–6
- grow review inventory
- improve duplicate handling
- launch claim tools

### Months 6–9
- launch paid claim plans
- launch analytics v1
- improve local SEO

### Months 9–12
- launch promoted but labeled placements
- category partnerships
- lead workflows

## 10. Bottom Line

How you make money:
- claimed profile subscriptions,
- analytics,
- lead generation,
- clearly labeled promoted placement,
- B2B reputation tooling later.

Why it works:
- trust creates traffic,
- traffic creates demand,
- transparency creates defensibility.
