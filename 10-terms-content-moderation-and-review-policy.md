# Terms, Content Moderation, and Review Policy Draft

_Last updated: April 3, 2026_

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the platform rules for:
- user-generated reviews,
- owner replies,
- moderation,
- takedowns,
- entity claims,
- legal-sensitive content.

This is an operational draft and should be reviewed by counsel before production use.

## 2. Platform Position

The platform hosts user-submitted opinions and experiences about entities such as landlords, properties, businesses, clinics, and professionals.

The platform does not guarantee that every review is accurate.  
Users are responsible for the content they submit.

## 3. Reviews We Allow

Allowed reviews generally include:
- honest personal experiences,
- quality or fairness opinions,
- communication/service feedback,
- pricing/value feedback,
- timeliness, cleanliness, professionalism, responsiveness,
- recommendation or non-recommendation based on experience.

Preferred style:
- “In my experience…”
- “I felt…”
- “I would not recommend because…”
- “The deposit was not returned in my case…”

## 4. Content We Restrict or Remove

We may restrict, redact, hide, or remove content that includes:
- hate speech,
- harassment,
- threats,
- obscenity outside policy limits,
- private personal information,
- impersonation,
- spam,
- duplicate/repeat manipulation,
- unsupported criminal accusations,
- incitement,
- clearly fabricated claims,
- content unrelated to the entity.

## 5. Legal-Sensitive Content

The platform should use extra caution with:
- allegations of crime,
- sexual misconduct claims,
- terrorism/extremism accusations,
- fraud accusations framed as fact,
- medical allegations,
- highly identifying personal data.

Operational rule:
Opinion-based experience is safer than hard factual criminal accusation.

Example safer phrasing:
- “I had a bad experience and would avoid.”
- “In my experience, the charges felt misleading.”

Example high-risk phrasing:
- “This person is a criminal.”
- “This doctor is a terrorist.”
- “This landlord runs illegal operations.”

High-risk content should be escalated or removed under policy.

## 6. Reviewer Responsibilities

By posting, users agree:
- to write honestly,
- not to impersonate,
- not to submit coordinated fake reviews,
- not to post private/sensitive data without authorization,
- not to use the platform for extortion or threats,
- not to publish unlawful content.

## 7. One Review Per Entity Rule

Core MVP rule:
- one account may keep only one active review per entity,
- edits are allowed subject to history,
- repeat spam is not allowed.

## 8. Community Moderation Signals

Users may:
- mark reviews helpful,
- signal similar experience,
- flag possible fake reviews,
- report policy violations.

These signals inform ranking and moderation but should not automatically replace human review in sensitive cases.

## 9. Owner Rights

Reviewed parties may:
- claim profiles,
- respond publicly,
- report reviews for policy violation,
- dispute identity mismatch,
- request duplicate merge.

Reviewed parties may not:
- silently delete valid reviews,
- directly edit review text,
- hide criticism merely because it is negative.

## 10. Takedown / Reporting Process

A complaint process should exist for:
- privacy violations,
- doxxing,
- impersonation,
- wrong-entity association,
- dangerous accusations,
- court-order compliance,
- clear policy violations.

Potential outcomes:
- keep as-is,
- redact private data,
- relabel under verification,
- remove,
- suspend account,
- merge or correct entity.

## 11. Appeals

Users and claimed owners should have a limited appeal path for:
- removed reviews,
- rejected claim requests,
- wrong-entity merges,
- sanctions.

## 12. Transparency Principle

Trust depends on fairness and visibility.  
The platform should prefer:
- transparent labels,
- visible replies,
- clear moderation reasons,
- audit history internally.

## 13. Legal and Compliance Note

Launch operations should be reviewed against Pakistan’s legal framework, including the Prevention of Electronic Crimes Act, 2016 and the Defamation Ordinance, 2002, both available through Pakistan Code. The operational consequence is simple: build clear moderation rules, process lawful complaints, preserve audit trails, and obtain legal review before public launch.

References:
- Pakistan Code — Prevention of Electronic Crimes Act, 2016
- Pakistan Code — Defamation Ordinance, 2002

## 14. Draft Public Terms Language Starter

Suggested starting language:

> Reviews and ratings on this platform reflect the opinions and experiences of users. We do not guarantee that every submission is accurate, complete, or current. Users are solely responsible for the content they submit. We may moderate, label, redact, or remove content that violates our policies or applicable law. Profile owners may respond publicly and may report content for review, but valid reviews are not removed solely because they are negative.

## 15. Operating Rule for Launch

When in doubt:
- protect privacy,
- prefer opinion framing,
- preserve both sides where possible,
- escalate high-risk allegations,
- keep clear records.
